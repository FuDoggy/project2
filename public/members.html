Spirit Guide

Getting to know your liquor

Vodka

Varieties originated in Russia, Poland, and Sweden.

  • Traditionally made by fermenting the distilled liquid from cereal grains.
  • Current distillers use potatoes and/or fruit as the base liquid.
  • Typically consumed neat and served freezer chilled.

Whiskey

The birthplace of American whiskey can be traced back to Virginia, Maryland and Pennsylvania. In 1791, whiskey began to be brewed as a rye based product.

  • Corn, the main ingredient of whiskey and makes up 51% of its ingredients, was plentiful in its origin areas.
  • Divided into two common brands: sour mash and bourbon.
  • In 1897, the "Bottled In Bond" Act was passed into law stating that whiskey must be straight (50% alcohol by volume) and produced by one distillation season under one distiller and on the grounds of one distillery.
  • It also must have been stored in a federally bonded warehouse under the supervision of the U.S. Government for at least four years. This bonded whiskey still holds a reputation for being the best of the best.

Gin

Gin, in its most basic terms, is a liquor of approximately 40% alcohol by volume (80 proof) or greater that is derived from grain distillation and primarily flavored with juniper berries (or juniper extract).

  • Gin was originally produced as a medicine distributed by “chemists” for the treatment of ailments such as gout and dyspepsia..
  • Certain types of gin can be enjoyed neat or on the rocks, or blended in a cocktail.
  • Many distilleries have been producing the same type of gin since the early 1700s.

Tequila

Tequila is made from the blue agave plant, primarily in the Jaliscan Highlands of central western Mexico. Mexican laws state that tequila can only be produced in the state of Jalisco and limited municipalities in the states of Guanajuato, Michoacan, Nayarit, and Tamaulipas.

  • Tequila is commonly served neat in Mexico and as a shot with salt and lime in the rest of the world.
  • It is known for its sweet, fruity , and earthy flavor.
  • Tequila must have between 35% and 55% alcohol content (70-110 US proof). It must contain at least 40% (80 US proof) alcohol to be sold in the US and Canada.

Rum

Rum is made by fermenting and then distilling sugarcane molasses or sugarcane juice, traditionally aged in oak barrels.

  • Most rums are made in the Caribbean, and North and South America.
  • Rums are produced in various grades, with light rums commonly used in cocktails, where golden and dark rums are served neat, straight, on the rocks, or used in cooking.
  • The alcohol concentration of rum can range from 20%, as in coconut rum, 40% most commonly, and 75.5%, like in Bacardi 151.
  • Some of the popular drinks containing rum are: The Daiquiri, Mojito, Pina Colada, and Cuba Libre.
